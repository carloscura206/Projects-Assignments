1A Explain how to turn P(E|H) to P(H|E), with E=Evidence and H=Hypothesis in layman's terms¶

Steps:

  1. Initially : Think of the initial hypothesis we need to predict (assuming an equal proability of previous hypothesis happening)
  2. Apply bayes theorem to update our perspective on the hypoethesis being true based on specific evidence shown. --Bayes Theorem: P(H|E) = P(E|H) * P(H) / P(E)

It allows us to change our starting confidence based on updated evidence that allows us to change how we perceive/predict things now. New evidence is shown which changes our perspective, Updated information helps us change our perspective. Based on previous evdience shown, you have an initial prediction(Hypothesis) on whats coming. But now you're given supported/updated information causing you now to hypothesize something else, and now want to see if your hypothesis has come true. given the new,current evidence (P(H|E))

Steps:

1. Prior P(H)
    -This is your starting belief of a hypothesis before taking into account of new evidence showcased
2. Likelihood P(E|H)
    -The chance of seeing the evidence if the hypothesis holds true
3. Posterior P(H|E)
    -This is now your adapted(updated) belief now folllowing the hypothesis after you take into consideration the new evidence shown

To convert from P(E|H) to P(H|E), the conversion factor pursues updating our starting belief P(H) based on new evidence P(E) shown. Bayes theorem acts as the conversion factor to convert our starting belief from the likelihood to end up updating into the posterior.
